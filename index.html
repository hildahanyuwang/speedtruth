SpeedTruth

Where are posted speed limits misaligned with real-world road conditions, putting vulnerable road users at risk, and where should a transport ministry invest first?

ADB · AI for Safer Roads 2026 · Thailand & Maharashtra (India)

The result in four numbers

14,711road segments scored on Safe-System survivable speed
5,973operate >30 km/h above a survivable speed (Critical)
4,017fast segments beside a school or market (median ~52% pedestrian fatality risk)
~13–14×benefit-cost of the matching iRAP-India interventions

The headline is not that drivers speed, on the priority corridors they often travel below the limit. It is that the limit itself is set for a road that no longer exists: a bypass posted at highway speed that informal traffic and roadside development have grown into.

What makes it trustworthy

  • We audited our own model and rebuilt it where the data did not support it (12 documented fixes).
  • We automated iRAP-style road-condition coding with a vision model, validated at 90% agreement with blind human rating, and used it to quantify our own threshold's weakness rather than hide it.
  • Ground-truthed against street imagery, blind and against controls (79% agreement); the model's "safe" calls are reliable.
  • Honest about the invisible fleet: informal three-wheelers and unhelmeted riders the probe data cannot see, so our scores on those corridors are a conservative lower bound.
